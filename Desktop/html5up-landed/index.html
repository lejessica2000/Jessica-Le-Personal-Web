Life at College Park

Expected Graduation: 2022

I'm a Computer Science major, and both a General Business and Technology Entrepreneurship minor at the University of Maryland, College Park! I enjoy learning about the business-related aspects of production, as well as the technicalities of entering a market/ finding a niche for one's own business. Having the opportunity to delve into so many topics is extremely rewarding, and I am always excited to expand my knowledege! As a member of the Global Communities living-learning program at UMD, I am also always thinking about the effects of an increasingly globalized world on technology and business.

In my free time, I organize for Technica, work as an Ambassador for the Maryland Center for Women in Computing (MCWIC), and help raise money for St. Jude with my sorority, Tri Delta. I hope to work as a Software Engineer and relocate to somewhere new (New York, Boston, and Seattle are my dream cities) after graduation. Follow below for my professional experiences and hobbies!

Maryland Center for Women in Computing

Teaching Ambassador

I've worked as an Ambassador for MCWIC since my freshman year at Maryland. My duties include helping CompSci Connect students complete projects using Scratch, Lego Mindstorm Robots, VR, and more. We also hold other workshops for kids and UMD students themselves; our core focus is to encourage students to pursue Computer Science, and to maintain/expand their initial interests in the field. Projects

My favorite projects at UMD have been the Submit Server Stimulation and NFA/DFA project. The Submit Server project was in Java; I am especially fond of this project because it was the first in which I had to come up with my own implementation (how I would store students' extra credit, etc.). This project showed me the importance of choosing appropriate data structures and keeping my code organized. The NFA/DFA project was in OCaml; though this was one of my more difficult projects, I enjoyed the challenge of translating conversions of NFAs, DFAs, and Regular Expressions into code. I have so far had experience working in Java, Ruby, and OCaml.

Miscellaneous: More about me and what I like!

My Vietnamese Roots

Fun Fact: My real name is Van Ha Le, and in Vietnam, you state people's names in the opposite order (Last, Middle, First). Because I was born in 2000 - the year of the dragon - my parents chose "Ha" to depict a dragon's ability to swim (means a body of water), and "Van" as a representation of the dragon's ability to fly (translates to "sky"). In high school, as I started to embrace my background, I chose to complete my Senior Capstone project on the Vietnam War (from the Vietnamese perspective). I didn't believe this point of view was adequately shown in school or in any texts in general; I interviewed parents from my old Vietnamese School and collected their stories to form a magazine!

UMD Alternative Breaks

Refugee and Immigrant Rights (Nashville, TN)

In Spring 2018, I went on one of UMD's Alternative Breaks. On each day of our trip, we visited 1-2 organizations working to support the immigrant and refugee population in Nashville (to provide housing, financial assistance, etc.); it was apparent that many of us felt a deep connection to this topic, as the majority of our group came from immigrant parents. It was painful to see how Nashville residents, who live in a very diverse melting pot, have experienced a lot of backlash from their own government.
